Dystopian Films Predicting the Future

With the pandemic flipping the world outside in, the numerous changes to society that has progressed over the last few months, may be here to stay. Frankly, I’m worried, mainly about my neighbours, and even myself, becoming too dependent on technology. Wall-E is a cute Disney movie which tells a fantastic story, while simultaneously giving us a glance at humanities future dystopia.

In Wall-E, humans live in futuristic lazy-boy supercomputers, existing solely in space, having abandoned the trash covered Earth. With driveable chairs and our Earth already being covered in trash, it doesn’t make living in space too far of a reach. Wall-E’s lazy-boy chairs come equipped with floating holographic screens situated right in front of the users face. With the pandemic forcing us inside, our regular face to face interactions have moved to online screens as well. Meeting online feels, and very well is, more efficient, however it also allows us to lower our standards, as we only need to look presentable for limited time on a small laptop window. With meetings online and workers at home, and the addition of tech orientated careers consistently on the rise, more and more individuals, will spend their lives working in front of a screen. Plus, with an Amazon Alexa or Google Home, I’ll never even need to stand up to turn my lights on, maybe one day they’ll be able to cook me dinner as well. We already have supercomputers, movable chairs, voice activated commands and see-through screens. How long will it be before a big tech company like Amazon or Google puts all these features together? Maybe it’ll come free with an Amazon “SpaceHub” subscription…
