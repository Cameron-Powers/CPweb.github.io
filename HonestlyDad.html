Honestly Dad

Honestly Dad, I don’t want to hear from you until anything to do legally with Mom, is over.

You hang everything over our heads, the tuition money for Heather and me, a trial with Mom. You’re weighing us down while you spend your time at a fancy Muskoka cottage which isn’t even yours. If you truly love something, you got to let it go.

I have given you chance after chance and then yesterday you blatantly lied to me about the divorce. Mom has been keeping me well informed from the beginning, and I know she is being honest. I’m no longer a gullible fool, I know when people are misleading me. Unlike Mom, every question I asked you was meet with diffusion and deflection, without a single specific detail to share. Your answers were nothing more than jargon.

You blamed it all on “the process”, and followed with putting down others in attempt to justify your own actions. You never believe me when I say this, but Mom never speaks ill of you, and trust me, I never lie when I write.

There is something broken inside of me which will never be fixed, and all I ask for is some real support. Mom has driven me to and from Newmarket and Ottawa over a dozen times. When she drops me off, she fills my fridge and cupboards with groceries and other necessities, all before giving me a big hug and hitting the road all on her own, the same day. Have you done the same?

On the contrary, you keep egging me on with rich and fancy items. I don’t want to come to your girlfriend’s big fancy cottage. I would much rather spend time at a cute and cozy one surrounded by family and friends, regardless of there being jet skis or not

I honestly don’t even want to leave Ottawa, this weekend all my buddies left to go home for the long weekend, and I barely knew what to do with myself. Ottawa is my Home.

I feel ready to take on the world. Work has been slow to start up this summer, and this weekend is the last pause before I shift into 5th gear. I am going to divert all my energy into my career goals, I need to give it my all. I could not be more excited for post covid Cam. Let’s gooooooo

I am loving how much I have been feeling myself lately, even when I was baked on the coach watching a show with my roommate Ali, I realized how good my legs look in sweatpants. I have been working out consistently, making me feel tight, fit, and healthy. Cameron is Cam’s biggest fan, my younger self would be so proud of me, but I can’t falter now. This is the moment when I need to give it my all.

The marriage was dysfunctional, among many other things, yet Heather and I would not be here today without it. There is always a positive to be found, and as Heather and I stand on tipping points of our individual success, the fence between us will limit your view. You will never win someone over with money and gifts, only energy and time. You never fought for Mom; I need to see you fight for Heather and me. Will you wither under the pressure of owning up to your mistakes? Or will you rise to the occasion?
