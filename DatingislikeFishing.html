Dating is like Fishing

We’ve all heard the cliché phrase, “there are plenty of fish in the sea”. (An expression my friend Jessie often rebuttals with the fact there is plenty of trash in the sea also...) However, there is accuracy and behind this unencouraging and over-used remark. Dating truly is a lot like fishing, it can sometimes be tiresome and boring, moving from place to place hoping for an opportunistic choice. Both dating and fishing include a lot of waiting and repeatedly throwing out bait before a beautiful catch bites.

Fishing and dating are all about location. A bar or house party is like fishing in a small quiet pond or lake. It’s a lot more casual from the start, where you will cast out a single line repeatedly before you get a catch. On the contrary, online dating apps such as Tinder are a lot like industrial fishing out in the ocean, where you throw a massive net into the water and just see what you get. Although it's bad for the environment, it’s effective to quickly obtain results with the ability to pick and choose from what you catch.

Regardless of where you are fishing, there will always be small and big catches. Many people who would be considered a big catch for various reason and more times than not, they know any fisher would be happy to have them on their line. These are the elusive big fish, you hear stories of them, but only actually see them out in the wild from time to time. Every fisher has their own technique, instead of casting their reel into the open waters, they may post an attractive photo of themselves online, setting the bait, for any hungry fish to go in for a bite.

Dating can definitely be considered a sport in my mind. It takes years of practice and training and it’s all about how many times you get back up from being shot down. Many fish for sport in their younger years, with the goal to get as many, and the best catches possible. A typical start to a career for any fisher, however as the years go by, many transition into fishing with the goal of catching a fish, that's not the biggest and best, but one that makes them the happiest.
